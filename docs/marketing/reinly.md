# Reinly — Brand Concept

*A clinic management system for the rest of us.*

---

## The Name

**Reinly** /ˈraɪn.li/ — *RYNE-lee*

From the German **rein** (pure, clean, unmixed) + **-ly** (purely, simply). It reads as a quiet promise: *purely the clinic software you need. Nothing more. Nothing dressed up.*

Two soft syllables. Doesn't shout. Sits well on a clinic door, a receipt, an SMS.

---

## The Problem We're Built Against

Most clinic software is **too much** — bloated suites built for hospital chains, sold with steep onboarding fees, locked into long contracts, charging per seat per module per integration. Small clinics, solo practitioners, and growing groups end up paying for features they'll never use, or worse, running their practice on spreadsheets and LINE chats because the "real" software costs more than rent.

The other end of the market is **too little** — free tools that crash at month-end, lose patient records, and have no support when things break.

There's a missing middle: **honest, complete, affordable.**

That's Reinly.

---

## Brand Pillars

**1. Lean by design**
Every feature exists because clinics asked for it. No upsell tiers, no "premium add-ons" for things that should be standard. If it's in Reinly, it works for everyone.

**2. Honest pricing**
One transparent price. No per-seat traps. No setup fees. No "contact sales for a quote." You should be able to see what it costs before you give us your email.

**3. Calm in the chaos**
Clinics are stressful. The software shouldn't add to it. Reinly is designed to be the quietest thing in the room — clear screens, no notification spam, no dark patterns.

**4. Built for small, not scaled-down enterprise**
Reinly isn't a stripped-down version of hospital software. It's purpose-built for clinics with 1–20 staff. The flows, the defaults, the language — all shaped around how small practices actually work.

**5. Yours to leave**
Export everything, anytime. Free. Your patients are yours, not ours. We earn your subscription every month, not by locking you in.

## Voice & Tone

**Plain, warm, professional. Like a competent friend who happens to know clinics.**

| Reinly sounds like | Reinly doesn't sound like |
|---|---|
| "Schedule's set. See you tomorrow." | "🎉 Appointment successfully scheduled!" |
| "One price. No surprises." | "Contact sales for enterprise pricing!" |
| "Built for small clinics." | "The leading AI-powered healthcare platform!" |
| "Cancel anytime, in two clicks." | "12-month commitment required." |
| "Your data stays yours." | "Industry-leading data ecosystem!" |

**Writing rules**
- Short sentences. Plain words.
- Talk to the doctor, the receptionist, the clinic owner — not "stakeholders."
- No medical jargon we don't need. No tech jargon at all.
- Never say "revolutionary," "AI-powered," "seamless," "robust."
- Localize properly. Thai users get real Thai, not Google Translate Thai.

## Pricing Philosophy *(critical to the brand)*

This is where Reinly's brand becomes real.

**One plan. One price. Everything included.**

Suggested model:
- **฿XXX / month per clinic location** (not per user)
- Unlimited staff, unlimited patients, unlimited appointments
- All features included from day one
- 14-day free trial, no credit card required
- Cancel anytime — full data export, free

**What we don't do:**
- ❌ Per-user/per-seat pricing
- ❌ "Starter / Pro / Enterprise" tiers that gate basic features
- ❌ Setup fees
- ❌ Onboarding fees
- ❌ Annual lock-in contracts
- ❌ "Talk to sales" pages
- ❌ Surprise overage charges

**Why this matters for the brand:** Every page, every email, every conversation reinforces that Reinly is the *fair* option. Pricing isn't just a business model — it's a brand promise.

## Core Features (Lean Scope)

These are what Reinly does, and the line we hold against feature creep:

**The essentials, done well:**
1. **Appointments** — calendar, booking, reminders (SMS/LINE/email)
2. **Patient records** — clinical notes, history, attachments, allergies
3. **Billing & payments** — invoices, receipts, e-tax, payment links
4. **Prescriptions** — drug database, dosing, printable & digital
5. **Inventory** — drug & supply tracking, low-stock alerts
6. **Reports** — revenue, patient flow, staff performance
7. **Multi-clinic** — for small groups, simple branch management

**What we deliberately leave out (for now, or forever):**
- Hospital-grade modules (OR scheduling, ICU, lab integration with national systems)
- Insurance claim processing for complex multi-payer environments
- AI diagnosis (out of scope, not our promise)
- Patient-facing telehealth platform (we integrate, we don't compete)

This restraint is the brand.

## Visual Identity

**Logo direction**
Lowercase wordmark: **reinly**
Custom letterforms, soft humanist edges. Optional mark: a single quiet symbol — perhaps a small horizontal line (a pause, a breath), or a thin rounded square (a clean room).

Avoid: medical crosses, stethoscopes, heart-EKG lines, blue-on-white "trust" tropes. Reinly should look like a thoughtful product, not generic healthcare.

**Color palette**

| Role | Color | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Bone | `#F5F2EC` | App background, paper-feel |
| Ink | Slate | `#1F2328` | Text, primary actions |
| Accent | Sage | `#9CAE9F` | CTAs, links, highlights |
| Calm | Mist | `#E6E2DA` | Cards, dividers |
| Signal | Clay | `#C97F5C` | Errors, urgent only |
| Soft | Cream | `#FAF7F1` | Surfaces |

A clinic palette without the clinical coldness. Warm, calm, easy on tired eyes during a 12-hour shift.

**Typography**
- **Display / brand:** Humanist serif — *GT Sectra*, *Source Serif*, or for Thai: *IBM Plex Sans Thai Looped* paired with English serif
- **UI / body:** Clean sans — *Inter* or *IBM Plex Sans* (excellent Thai support)
- **Numbers:** Tabular figures everywhere — billing, dashboards, schedules need this

**Imagery**
- Real clinics, real people, soft natural light
- Hands writing, hands on keyboards, hands on patient files
- Thai/Southeast Asian clinics, not stock-photo white-coat doctors in Western hospitals
- Empty waiting rooms in morning light, neat desks, organized shelves
- Avoid: aggressive blue gradients, 3D molecule renders, "futuristic" UI mockups

## Brand Triangulation

Reinly sits where these worlds meet:

- **Linear** (calm, opinionated software) +
- **Stripe** (developer-grade trust, clean documentation) +
- **Wise** (transparent pricing as marketing) +
- **Muji** (anti-brand restraint) +
- **Cliniko / SimplePractice** (small-clinic SaaS done well, in English markets)

Reinly is the version of these built for **Thai and Southeast Asian small clinics**, where the legacy options are either expensive Western imports or feature-stuffed local platforms charging hospital-grade prices.

---

## What Reinly Is Not

- Not for hospitals
- Not for chains over 20 locations
- Not "AI-first" (we'll use AI where it disappears into the work, never as marketing)
- Not a marketing platform pretending to be a clinic system
- Not free (free SaaS is unsustainable, and unsustainable software hurts clinics most)
- Not cheap-feeling — fair price, premium feel

## Brand Story (one paragraph)

> Reinly started with a question: why does clinic software cost as much as a junior staff salary? We looked at the market and saw two extremes — bloated hospital systems that small clinics can't afford, and free tools that lose patient records on busy days. So we built the missing middle: a clinic management system that does the essentials beautifully, at a price small practices can actually pay, with no contracts and no hidden fees. Less, on purpose. Reinly.
