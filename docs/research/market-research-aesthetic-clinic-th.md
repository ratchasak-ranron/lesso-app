## 3. Common Feature Pattern (across all vendors)

Baseline expected by buyers:

- Patient records + visit notes (SOAP-style)
- Appointment booking (online + walk-in)
- Course/package/voucher tracking (aesthetic-critical)
- Commission tracking per doctor/service
- LINE OA integration (TH cultural baseline)
- Multi-branch dashboard
- Member points/loyalty
- Inventory (consumables, products)
- Sales analytics

## 4. Aesthetic-Specific Must-Haves

From competitor scans:

- **Course usage tracking** — 10-session package burn-down, visible to staff at counter
- **Before/after photo storage** per visit (PDPA-compliant)
- **Doctor commission per service** (variable rates)
- **Treatment consent forms** (e-signature ideal)
- **PDPA compliance** — TH legal requirement (consent, audit log, data export, retention)

---

## 5. Identified Gaps (Differentiation Wedges)

Where competitors fall short — candidate positioning angles:

1. **Mobile-first / tablet-first UX** — TH competitors are mostly desktop-web, clunky on iPad at counter
2. **LINE-native flow** — booking via LINE OA without separate patient app
3. **Self-serve onboarding** — paper→system in <1 day with templates; most require sales call
4. **Self-serve pricing & signup** — most TH vendors gate pricing behind demo request
5. **Multi-branch chain ops** — cross-branch member portability, course usage at any branch
6. **Marketing automation** — recall campaigns triggered by visit gap, package expiry alerts
7. **Modern UI** — most TH systems look 2010-era; aesthetic clinic owners care about polish

---

## 6. Market Dynamics (User Input)

- **Red ocean** — many competitors, price-sensitive market
- **Branch expansion is growth strategy** — customers select clinics near home → multi-branch software is competitive moat
- **Buyer profile** — small clinic owner, cost-conscious, currently on paper/Excel
- **Pain points cited** — Excel chaos, existing software too expensive

## 7. Regulatory Context

- **PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act, B.E. 2562)** — TH data protection law
  - Consent management required
  - Audit log of data access
  - Data export on subject request
  - Retention policy enforcement
- **Multi-language** — Thai primary, English secondary (for SEA expansion + intl clinic staff)
- **Currency** — THB primary, multi-currency optional for tourist clinics

---

## 8. Strategic Recommendation

### Reframe Positioning

"50% cheaper than DoctorEase" alone = head-on collision with Cliniclive/Miracle.

**Stronger positioning candidates**:

- **A**: "DoctorEase features at Miracle price + modern UX" — quality-on-budget play
- **B**: "LINE-first clinic OS for chains" — multi-branch + LINE focus, vertical wedge
- **C**: "Open-source-style transparency, aesthetic-clinic-specific" — niche + trust

Recommendation: **B** or hybrid B+A. User explicitly mentioned multi-branch as competitive driver.
